Brief History

1986 - 1st mention in Harvard Business Review article

1990s - integrating ideas into a single framework, testing and improving it

2002 - becoming worldwide spread

2009 - publication of The Scrum Guide

What is Scrum?

a lightweight framework that helps people, teams and organizations generate value through adaptive solutions for complex problems

Scrum Team

Product Owner

  • builds and manages the product backlog
  • ensures that the product backlog is transparent, visible and understood
  • prioritizes the work to be done
  • a single person

Scrum Team

Scrum Master

  • coaches developers, product owners, and the business on the scrum process
  • helps the team optimize their transparency and delivery flow
  • ensures that all events take place, are positive, productive, and kept within the timebox

Scrum Team

Developers

  • self-organized, small team
  • cross-functional
  • no titles and sub-teams

Scrum Artifacts

Product Backlog - primary list of features, enhancements, and fixes that need to get done

Sprint Backlog:

  • sprint goal (why)
  • items selected for the current sprint (what)
  • plan for delivering the result (how)

Increment - complete, fully functioning output of the sprint in a usable condition

Scrum Events

Sprint

  • Participants: the entire Scrum Team
  • Timebox: 1-4 weeks
  • Result: increment

Scrum Events

Sprint Planning

  • Participants: the entire Scrum Team
  • Timebox: maximum 8 hours
  • Topics:
    • Why is this sprint valuable?
    • What can be done in this sprint?
    • How will the chosen work get done?

Scrum Events

Daily scrum / Stand-up

  • Participants: Developers, sometimes also the Product Owner or the Scrum Master
  • Timebox: maximum 15 minutes
  • Purposes:
    • to inspect progress toward the sprint goal
    • to produce a plan for the next day
    • to adapt the sprint backlog if necessary

Scrum Events

Sprint Review

  • Participants: the entire Scrum Team, stakeholders
  • Timebox: maximum 4 hours
  • Purposes:
    • to show the items that are already done and get feedback
    • to collaborate on what to do next
    • to adapt the product backlog if necessary

Scrum Events

Sprint Retrospective

  • Participants: the entire Scrum Team
  • Timebox: maximum 3 hours
  • Purposes:
    • to discuss how the work went during the spring
    • to plan ways to increase quality and effectiveness
